## Packaging & Color Psychology - Comprehensive Guide

### Color Psychology for Supplements & Health Products

Based on packaging research and consumer psychology studies:

#### RED - Excitement, Passion, Boldness
**Emotions/Values:** Excitement, bold, youthful, passion, zeal, warmth, strength, power, action, enthusiasm, love, desire
**Best For:** Energy products, impulse purchases, youthful brands
**Usage in Supplements:** Limited - may feel aggressive for health products unless targeting younger demographics
**Brand Examples:** Coca-Cola, Netflix, LEGO

#### ORANGE - Warmth, Friendliness, Creativity
**Emotions/Values:** Friendly, cheerful, confident, fun, adventurous, warm, cost-effective, creative
**Best For:** Playful, social, youthful personalities; approachable brands
**Usage in Supplements:** Can convey affordability and fun, but needs careful handling to avoid "cheap" perception
**Brand Examples:** Fanta, Firefox
**Note:** Less commonly used than red/blue - offers differentiation opportunity

#### YELLOW - Optimism, Energy, Visibility
**Emotions/Values:** Cheerful, clarity, warmth, optimism, fun, energy, happiness, confidence
**Best For:** Approachability, forward-thinking, youthful brands (kids/teens focus)
**Usage in Supplements:** Great accent color; effective in combination (e.g., Subway's yellow+green = energy + freshness)
**Brand Examples:** McDonald's, DHL, Subway
**Note:** Pure yellow often skews younger; mixed palettes broaden appeal

#### GREEN - Nature, Health, Harmony ⭐ KEY FOR SUPPLEMENTS
**Emotions/Values:** Soothing, freshness, growth, peaceful, healthy, eco-friendly, harmony, security, wealth
**Best For:** Organic, eco-friendly, health-forward products (vitamin/nutrient category)
**Usage in Supplements:** MOST COMMON COLOR - signals natural, organic, sustainable
**Nuances:**
- Bright greens = fresh, energetic
- Deep greens = stability, luxury
- Olive/earthy greens = grounding, rustic, authenticity
**Warning:** Avoid "greenwashing" - must back green with real sustainability
**Brand Examples:** Subway, Starbucks, Starbucks

#### BLUE - Trust, Intelligence, Calm ⭐ KEY HEALTHCARE COLOR
**Emotions/Values:** Calming, trustworthy, efficiency, serenity, logic, intelligence, dependability, strength, honesty
**Best For:** Financial, tech, healthcare, pharmaceutical - SAFEST, MOST TRUSTED color
**Usage in Supplements:** Ideal for medical/science-backed claims; conveys reliability and professionalism
**Brand Examples:** Varies across healthcare, pharmaceutical
**Note:** Can reduce perceived "tastiness" but increases healthiness perception

#### PURPLE - Luxury, Indulgence, Spirituality
**Emotions/Values:** Indulgence, luxury, opulence, spirituality, nobility, wisdom, exclusivity
**Best For:** Premium, high-end, artistic, spiritual supplements
**Usage in Supplements:** Suggests exceptional quality, exclusivity; great for differentiating from green/brown clutter
**Brand Examples:** Premium beauty/artistic brands
**Note:** Especially effective with gold/silver accents

#### TURQUOISE - Calmness, Purity, Clarity
**Emotions/Values:** Calmness, purity, clarity
**Best For:** Clean, pure, calming products

#### PINK - Femininity, Beauty, Playful
**Emotions/Values:** Femininity, calming, beauty, youthful, playful, love
**Best For:** Women-targeted beauty, skin, hair supplements
**Usage in Supplements:** Clearly signals female demographic; beauty focus
**Brand Examples:** Many women's supplement brands

#### BROWN - Earthy, Natural, Comforting
**Emotions/Values:** Earthy, comforting, security, natural
**Best For:** Traditional, Ayurvedic, grounding products
**Usage in Supplements:** Common in Ayurvedic/organic but can blend into category clutter
**Note:** Many supplement bottles are green or brown - consider differentiation

#### BLACK - Elegance, Premium, Sophistication
**Emotions/Values:** Elegance, premium, sophistication, authority, strength, mystery
**Best For:** Luxury positioning, strong authority statements
**Usage in Supplements:** Premium segment differentiation

#### WHITE - Simplicity, Purity, Clean
**Emotions/Values:** Simplicity, elegance, purity, innocence, goodness, humility, cleanliness, new beginnings, premium
**Best For:** Clean, minimal design; clinical purity
**Usage in Supplements:** Common base color; conveys simplicity and purity

#### GREY - Calm, Neutral, Balanced
**Emotions/Values:** Calm, neutral, balanced
**Usage in Supplements:** Can make packaging feel conservative or masculine

### Key Consumer Research Insights

1. **Taste vs Health Trade-off:**
   - Warm colors (red, orange) → Increased taste and indulgence expectations
   - Cool colors (blue, green) → Increased healthiness and sustainability perception
   - Cool colors can diminish perceived "tastiness"

2. **Vice vs Virtue Products:**
   - Indulgent treats ("vice") → Warm colors boost purchase intent
   - Health-oriented products ("virtue") → Cool colors more compelling

3. **"Sensation Transference":**
   - Consumers transfer feelings about packaging onto the product itself
   - Identical formulas in different colors → completely different reactions

4. **Category Considerations:**
   - Don't ignore category norms completely (can confuse consumers)
   - "Off-category" colors can be memorable and differentiating
   - Must be deliberate and supported by branding cues (logo, typography, messaging)

5. **Best Practices:**
   - Limit to 2-3 complementary or contrasting hues
   - Work from emotions/brand promises first, then pick colors
   - Test in real-world lighting and formats
   - Consider legibility requirements for text

### Indian Market Specific Insights

1. **WOW Skin Science/HK Vitals (Amber & Gold):**
   - Most products in India packaged in white
   - WOW chose amber and gold to stand out and capture consumer attention
   - Gold creates premium feel, amber suggests authenticity/earthiness
   - Amber/gold combination stands out significantly compared to white packaging

2. **Supplement Packaging Trends (India):**
   - Moving towards streamlined, "clean" design vs heavy color and content
   - Gradients and shiny effects being dropped for flatter design
   - Natural ingredients on packaging with Sanskrit script or illustrations for authenticity
   - Durable and sustainable materials preferred
   - Consistent brand colors and fonts across packaging

3. **Gummy-Specific Packaging:**
   - Individual pillow packaging for each gummy is standard in India (prevents sticking in hot weather)
   - Jars for bulk packaging
   - Individual wrappers emphasized as a feature (convenience for carrying anywhere)

- **Mid-segment (₹500-₹800):** Most competition here - Be Bodywise, Carbamide Forte
- **Premium segment (₹1000+):** ORGANIC INDIA, Wellbeing Nutrition

### Packaging Opportunities:
- **Stand-out colors:** Amber + Gold (WOW approach) works well in Indian market
- **Purple** - Rare in supplements, signals luxury/spirituality - great for Ashwagandha (medicinal/spiritual herb)
- **Green + Gold** combination - Natural + premium
- **Avoid:** Green/brown only (cluttered category), white only (blends in)

### Messaging Strategy Insights:
- Stress KSM-66's full-spectrum quality (like ORGANIC INDIA)
- Emphasize simplicity ("Just one gummy a day" - tested messaging)
- Lead with benefits (stress relief, energy, sleep) over ingredients
- Social proof critical (customer testimonials with names/details)
- Consider gender-neutral vs women-only targeting

### One4Health Positioning Considerations:
- **Starting locally (Lucknow) → Going national online**
- Ashwagandha KSM-66 (300mg) - similar to ORGANIC INDIA dosage
- Sugar-free, vegan, gluten-free + Vitamin D2 (added value)
- New brand vs established competition
- **Key Differentiator Opportunity:** Purple + Gold packaging, storytelling around Lucknow/northern India sourcing, or premium quality at mid-segment pricing
